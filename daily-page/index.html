Lead Story

Oil Reclaims Control of the Tape as Relief Trade Gives Way to Inflation Stress

The market shifted back into an energy-shock regime after the prior session's relief rally faded. Equities weakened, the dollar firmed, and Treasury yields rose alongside crude, pointing to a stagflation-style repricing rather than a clean flight to safety.

Thursday, April 2, 2026, is defined by the same cross-asset message: higher oil is once again overpowering the growth narrative. Asia sold off harder than the U.S., European risk sentiment softened at the open, and U.S. futures turned lower as markets reassessed geopolitical risk and inflation pressure together.

The key read-through is that the market is still taking its cue from energy and rates. That keeps this from feeling like a normal risk-off day. It looks more like a transition session where the inflation impulse is arriving faster than the safety bid, with the relief rally from Wednesday, April 1 already fading.

At a Glance

  • Brent pushed back above $108.
  • Nasdaq futures lagged the broader U.S. tape.
  • Treasury yields rose instead of acting as a haven.
  • Gold fell even as geopolitical stress stayed elevated.

Today's Frame

Not a clean risk-off day. The tape reads as inflation-first stress with energy driving the macro narrative and the relief trade already reversing.
