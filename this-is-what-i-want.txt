This is what I want.

The purpose is to scan world markets for:
- special situations
- catalyst trades
- bigger-than-normal moves in assets and equities globally
- early signs that something important is changing

The key idea is to avoid starting from normal headline flow.
I want to create news by asking the right market questions first.

The first questions should be:
- was the move bigger than normal
- was volume higher or lower than normal
- what moved, and on what volume
- is volatility up or down
- is volatility higher or lower than normal

The first screen should focus on:
- `USO` for oil direction, move size, and volume
- `VIXY` for volatility direction, move size, and volume
- `SPY` for broad equity-market direction, move size, and volume
- major sector indexes or sector ETFs

I want the base data and the significance of the move.

Examples of useful information:
- how unusual the move was versus the last year
- how unusual the trading volume was versus normal
- whether volatility confirms or contradicts the move

Important framing:
- volatility is a core way to measure risk
- the edition should think in terms of both return and risk
- not only what moved the most, but what looked best or worst given volatility

Things to refine later:
- exact section structure
- exact source list
- exact statistical thresholds for “bigger than normal”
- how much weight to give U.S. markets versus the rest of the world
- how much company-level news versus macro and cross-asset signals
